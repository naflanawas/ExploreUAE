1.Burj Khalifa

Burj Khalifa is the tallest building in the world, located in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. It stands at a height of 828 meters (2,716 feet) and has 163 floors above ground. The building was designed by the Chicago-based architecture and engineering firm of Skidmore, Owings & Merrill, and its construction was completed in 2009. Burj Khalifa features a mix of residential, commercial, and hotel spaces. The building's observation deck, called "At The Top," is located on the 124th floor and offers stunning views of the city. The building also has the world's highest outdoor observation deck on the 148th floor. In addition to its impressive height, Burj Khalifa is known for its innovative design and engineering. The building features a Y-shaped floor plan that is designed to maximize views and minimize wind forces on the tower. It also incorporates a number of sustainable design features, such as a high-performance curtain wall system and a condensate collection system that recycles water for irrigation and other uses. Overall, Burj Khalifa is an iconic landmark that has helped to transform Dubai into a global destination for tourism and business.

2.Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque

Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque is a prominent religious landmark located in Abu Dhabi, the capital city of the United Arab Emirates. It is one of the largest mosques in the world, with a capacity to hold over 41,000 worshippers at one time. The mosque was named after Sheikh Zayed bin Sultan Al Nahyan, the founder and first president of the UAE. It was designed by Syrian architect Yousef Abdelky and features a mix of traditional Islamic and modern architectural styles. The mosque's most distinctive features include its 82 domes, four minarets, and 24-carat gold gilded chandeliers. The mosque's courtyard is covered with floral designs in marble, while the main prayer hall is adorned with intricate floral and calligraphic designs on its walls and ceilings. The mosque is open to visitors of all faiths and is a popular tourist attraction. Visitors are required to dress modestly and remove their shoes before entering the mosque. Guided tours are also available to learn more about the mosque's history, architecture, and significance to the UAE's culture and identity. Overall, Sheikh Zayed Grand Mosque is a beautiful and important religious site that showcases the Islamic faith and Arab culture to the world.

4.Emirates Towers

Emirates Towers is a high-rise building complex located in Dubai, United Arab Emirates. The complex consists of two skyscrapers, known as Emirates Tower One and Emirates Tower Two, which are both over 300 meters tall. Emirates Tower One, the taller of the two buildings, stands at a height of 355 meters (1,165 feet) and has 56 floors. Emirates Tower Two, on the other hand, is slightly shorter at 309 meters (1,014 feet) and has 56 floors as well. The towers were designed by Norr Group Consultants International Ltd and were completed in 2000. They are primarily used for commercial purposes, with office spaces, conference facilities, and retail outlets located within the complex. Emirates Towers is known for its striking architecture, which features a modern design inspired by traditional Islamic elements. The towers are connected by a large podium that houses a variety of retail and dining options, as well as a luxury hotel known as Jumeirah Emirates Towers Hotel. Overall, Emirates Towers is a prominent landmark in Dubai's skyline and a symbol of the city's growth and development as a global business hub.
